# Shorts compliance

Every Shorts concept is run through the same disclaimer engine as long-form. The compliance posture is the same: disclose, do not deceive, and do not promise outcomes.

## Disclosures and disclaimers

- General educational disclaimer
- Financial disclaimer for any money / invest / tax / income / side hustle topic
- Medical disclaimer for any medical / mental / nutrition / fitness topic
- Legal disclaimer for any legal topic
- Affiliate disclosure for any description that includes an affiliate link
- Sponsorship disclosure for any paid placement
- AI content disclosure when AI voice or AI visuals are material to the Short
- Copyright caution when third-party footage is included
- Investment risk disclaimer for any investment topic
- Data accuracy disclaimer when statistics are quoted
- No-guaranteed-results disclaimer for any outcome-oriented claim
- Platform policy disclaimer reinforces alignment with YouTube community guidelines

## Copyrighted media rules

- Do not bundle copyrighted clips, music, or art without a documented license.
- Public domain footage requires an archive citation.
- Stock footage requires retained license terms.
- Screen recordings require permission of the recorded surface.

## AI disclosure

When a Short uses an AI voice clone, an AI-generated illustration, or other AI assets that are material to the content, the AI content disclosure runs in the description and pinned comment. The compliance engine adds it automatically when `aiGeneratedContent` is true.

## No misleading claims

The thumbnail and title engines strip clickbait phrases (`guaranteed`, `free money`, `overnight`, `shocking truth`, `banned`, `they don't want you to know`, `doctors hate this`, `one weird trick`, `destroyed`). The compliance engine flags scripts containing fake-engagement language as a high-severity issue.

## Copyright and licensing in the export bundle

Every export bundle includes the asset license requirement per scene. Long-form-plus-Shorts bundles inherit those requirements.
