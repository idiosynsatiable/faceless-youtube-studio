# Shorts monetization

Shorts are typically a discovery surface, not the primary revenue engine. The Shorts engine treats every Short as a funnel asset for the long-form anchor and the email list.

## Realistic expectations

- Shorts ad revenue per view is generally lower than long-form per view.
- High Short watch counts do not always translate into subscribers or product sales.
- Treat Shorts as the top-of-funnel; route every successful Short to the long-form video and the lead magnet.

## Monetization paths the funnel engine connects

- Long-form conversion: pinned comment + description + end-screen route to the long-form anchor
- Affiliate funnel: vetted-tool links in the description with the FTC-aligned disclosure block
- Email capture: free starter checklist relevant to the niche; capture point in description
- Sponsorship path: long-form sponsor segments built on top of the Shorts cluster
- Product ladder: free lead magnet -> low-ticket templates -> core course -> premium workshop -> high-touch service
- Channel membership: route Shorts viewers to a long-form trailer that pitches membership

## Disclosures we always insert when applicable

- Affiliate disclosure when description contains affiliate links
- Sponsorship disclosure when a sponsor segment is included
- Financial disclaimer when the Short discusses money, investing, taxes, side hustles, or income
- Medical disclaimer when the Short discusses medical, mental health, fitness, or nutrition
- Legal disclaimer when the Short discusses legal topics
- AI content disclosure when AI voice or AI-generated visuals are material
- No-guaranteed-results disclaimer for any outcome-oriented claim

## Forbidden monetization tactics

- Selling promised income, returns, or weight-loss outcomes
- Hidden affiliate placements without disclosure
- Sponsor segments that are not clearly disclosed
- Buying subscribers, likes, views, or comments
- Pay-to-engagement pods
